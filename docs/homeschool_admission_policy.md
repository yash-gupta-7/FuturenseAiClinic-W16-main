# Homeschool Admission Policy

**Document owner:** Office of Admissions
**Last updated:** Fall 2025

## 1. Eligibility
Homeschooled applicants are evaluated on the same academic standards as applicants from accredited high schools. The University welcomes homeschooled applicants and considers the full context of a self-directed education.

## 2. Standardized Testing
While the University is **test-optional for applicants from accredited high schools**, this test-optional policy does **not** extend to homeschooled applicants. **Homeschooled applicants must submit official SAT or ACT scores.** Standardized scores provide an external benchmark in the absence of an accredited transcript.

## 3. Required Documentation
In addition to test scores, homeschooled applicants must submit:

- A detailed **transcript or course-by-course record** listing subjects, texts used, and grades or evaluations.
- **Course descriptions** for core academic subjects.
- At least **two letters of recommendation**, with at least one from a non-family instructor (for example, a co-op teacher, tutor, dual-enrollment professor, or coach).

## 4. Dual Enrollment
Homeschooled applicants who completed college coursework through dual enrollment should submit official college transcripts; such credits are evaluated under the Transfer Credit Policy.

## 5. Interview
An optional interview is recommended for homeschooled applicants and may be requested through the Office of Admissions.
