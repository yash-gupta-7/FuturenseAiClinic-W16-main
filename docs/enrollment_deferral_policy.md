# Enrollment Deferral Policy

**Document owner:** Office of Admissions
**Last updated:** Fall 2025

## 1. Eligibility
Admitted, deposited students who wish to delay the start of their studies may request an enrollment deferral. Deferrals are granted for personal, professional, or medical reasons and are reviewed individually.

## 2. Length of Deferral
An enrollment deferral may be granted for **up to two semesters (one academic year)**. Requests for a longer delay require the applicant to submit a new application for a future term.

## 3. How to Request
A deferral request must be submitted in writing to the Office of Admissions by **July 1** for fall admits (or **December 1** for spring admits). The request must state the intended term of entry.

## 4. Enrollment Deposit
The **enrollment deposit is non-refundable but transfers** to the deferred term and is applied to the student's first-semester charges. Students who defer do not pay a second deposit.

## 5. Conditions
- Deferred students may **not** enroll at another degree-granting institution during the deferral period. Doing so voids the deferral and requires reapplication as a transfer student.
- Merit scholarships are honored for one deferral year unless the award letter states otherwise.
- Deferred international students must obtain an updated I-20 reflecting the new start date.

## 6. Military and Medical Exceptions
Students called to active military duty or facing a documented medical emergency may request a deferral beyond one year; such requests are reviewed by the Dean of Admissions.
