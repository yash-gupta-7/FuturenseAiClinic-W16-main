# Residency for Tuition Purposes Policy

**Document owner:** Office of the Registrar
**Last updated:** Fall 2025

## 1. Why Residency Matters
Tuition is charged at two rates: a lower **in-state (resident) rate** and a higher **out-of-state (non-resident) rate**. Residency classification is determined at admission and may be reclassified upon petition.

## 2. Establishing Residency
To qualify for in-state tuition, an applicant must demonstrate **physical domicile in the state for at least 12 consecutive months** immediately before the term of enrollment, with intent to remain (for example, state driver's license, voter registration, in-state employment, or lease/property records).

## 3. Dependent Students
A financially **dependent student's residency follows that of the parent or legal guardian** who claims them. If the parent is an established state resident, the dependent student is classified as in-state.

## 4. Special Classifications
- **Active-duty military** stationed in the state, and their dependents, qualify for in-state tuition regardless of the 12-month rule.
- Students qualifying under state **non-resident tuition exemption (e.g., certain graduates of in-state high schools)** may receive the in-state rate.

## 5. Reclassification
A non-resident student seeking reclassification must petition the Registrar with documentation before the term begins. Attending the University primarily as a student does **not** by itself establish domicile.
