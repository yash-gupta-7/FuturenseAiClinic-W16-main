# Waitlist Policy

**Document owner:** Office of Admissions
**Last updated:** Fall 2025

## 1. Purpose
The waitlist allows the University to manage enrollment when the number of qualified applicants exceeds available seats. A waitlist offer is not an offer of admission.

## 2. Accepting a Waitlist Place
Applicants offered a waitlist place must confirm their interest through the applicant portal by the date stated in their notification. Applicants who do not confirm are removed from the waitlist.

## 3. Additional Materials
Waitlisted applicants may submit **one additional letter of recommendation** to strengthen their file. The University does **not** accept additional essays, updated test scores, or supplemental portfolios from waitlisted applicants. A short note on significant new achievements (for example, a final-semester grade improvement) may be added through the portal.

## 4. Decision Timeline
Waitlist decisions are released on a rolling basis as seats become available, and **no later than May 15**. Waitlisted applicants who have not received an offer by that date are released from the waitlist.

## 5. Financial Aid for Waitlist Admits
Applicants admitted from the waitlist remain eligible for need-based aid but may have reduced access to institutional grant funds, which are largely committed by the priority deadline.
