# Disability Services and Accommodations Policy

**Document owner:** Disability Services Office (DSO)
**Last updated:** Fall 2025

## 1. Commitment
The University provides reasonable accommodations to qualified students with documented disabilities in accordance with the ADA and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act.

## 2. How to Register
Students must **register with the Disability Services Office** and submit documentation from a qualified professional describing the disability and its functional impact. Accommodations are **not retroactive** and begin only after registration and an intake meeting.

## 3. Common Accommodations
- Extended time and reduced-distraction settings for examinations.
- Accessible course materials and assistive technology.
- Note-taking support and recorded lectures.
- Accessible or modified housing assignments (coordinated with Residence Life).

## 4. Documentation Standards
Documentation should be current (generally within three years for most conditions) and issued by a licensed provider. The DSO may request additional information to determine appropriate accommodations.

## 5. Confidentiality
Disability documentation is maintained separately from the academic record and is shared with faculty only to the extent needed to implement approved accommodations.

## 6. Appeals
A student who disagrees with an accommodation decision may appeal in writing to the Director of Disability Services within 15 business days.
