# International Admission Policy

**Document owner:** Office of International Admissions
**Last updated:** Fall 2025

## 1. Scope
This policy applies to all applicants who require a student visa (F-1 or J-1) or who have completed their most recent academic credential outside the United States.

## 2. English Proficiency Requirements
All international applicants whose first language is not English must demonstrate English proficiency by submitting one of the following:

- **TOEFL iBT:** minimum total score of **80** for undergraduate applicants and **90** for graduate applicants.
- **IELTS Academic:** minimum overall band of **6.5**, with no individual band below 6.0.
- **Duolingo English Test:** minimum score of **110**.

### English proficiency waiver
The English proficiency requirement is waived if the applicant has completed a degree (or at least two full years of study) at an institution where English is the sole language of instruction. A waiver request must be submitted with supporting documentation from the prior institution.

## 3. Credential Evaluation
Applicants with non-U.S. transcripts must submit a course-by-course credential evaluation from a NACES-member agency (for example, WES or ECE). Evaluations must be sent directly to the Office of International Admissions.

## 4. Financial Documentation
Admitted international students must submit a Certificate of Finances and supporting bank statements showing sufficient funds for one academic year before an I-20 will be issued.

## 5. Application Deadlines
- Fall intake: **May 1**
- Spring intake: **October 1**

International applications received after these dates are reviewed on a space-available basis only.
