# Transfer Credit Policy

**Document owner:** Office of the Registrar
**Last updated:** Fall 2025

## 1. Eligibility
Transfer credit is awarded only for coursework completed at institutions that are **regionally accredited** by a U.S. Department of Education–recognized accreditor. Coursework from non-accredited institutions is **not accepted** for transfer credit, except through the portfolio prior-learning review described in Section 5.

## 2. Minimum Grade
Only courses completed with a grade of **C (2.0)** or higher are eligible for transfer. Courses graded pass/fail are eligible only when the institution certifies that "pass" represents a grade of C or better.

## 3. Maximum Transferable Credits
- A maximum of **60 semester credits** may be transferred toward a bachelor's degree.
- Of those, no more than **60 credits** may come from two-year (community) colleges. Credits beyond the community-college cap must be earned at a four-year institution.
- At least **30 credits** of the degree must be completed in residence at this University, regardless of transfer credit awarded.

## 4. Course Equivalency
Transfer courses are matched to University equivalents by the relevant academic department. Courses without a direct equivalent may be awarded general elective credit.

## 5. Prior-Learning Portfolio Review
Applicants seeking credit for learning from non-accredited institutions, military training, or professional certifications may petition for credit through a portfolio review assessed by the academic department. A non-refundable assessment fee applies.

## 6. Articulation Agreements
Students transferring from partner community colleges with an active articulation agreement may transfer credits under the terms of that agreement, which may exceed the standard equivalency mapping.
