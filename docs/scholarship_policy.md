# Merit Scholarship Policy

**Document owner:** Office of Scholarships
**Last updated:** Fall 2025

## 1. Award Basis
Merit scholarships are awarded at the time of admission based on academic record (GPA and, where submitted, standardized test scores). No separate application is required for most merit awards; competitive named scholarships require a supplemental essay.

## 2. Renewal Requirement
To renew a merit scholarship each year, a student must:

- Maintain a **cumulative GPA of at least 3.5**, and
- Enroll **full time (a minimum of 12 credits per semester)**.

Eligibility is reviewed at the **end of each academic year** (after spring grades post).

## 3. Grace Period
A student whose cumulative GPA falls below 3.5 is placed on **scholarship probation for one semester**. If the student raises the cumulative GPA to 3.5 or above by the end of the probation semester, the scholarship is reinstated. If not, the award is forfeited.

## 4. Duration
Merit scholarships are renewable for a maximum of **eight semesters** of undergraduate study. They do not apply to summer terms.

## 5. Stacking
Merit scholarships may be combined with need-based grants, but total institutional aid may not exceed the cost of tuition and mandatory fees.
