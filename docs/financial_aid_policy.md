# Financial Aid Policy

**Document owner:** Office of Financial Aid
**Last updated:** Fall 2025

## 1. How to Apply
All students seeking need-based aid must file the **Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA)** each academic year. The University's federal school code must be listed on the FAFSA.

## 2. Priority Deadline
The **priority FAFSA deadline is March 1**. Applications completed by this date receive first consideration for limited institutional grant funds. FAFSAs filed after March 1 are considered for federal aid only, on a funds-available basis.

## 3. Types of Aid
- **Federal grants** (e.g., Pell Grant) for eligible undergraduates.
- **Institutional need-based grants**, awarded by priority deadline.
- **Federal Direct Loans** (subsidized and unsubsidized).
- **Federal Work-Study**, subject to fund availability.

## 4. Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP)
To remain eligible for financial aid, students must maintain **Satisfactory Academic Progress**: a cumulative GPA of at least **2.0** and completion of at least **67%** of attempted credits. SAP is reviewed at the end of each academic year.

## 5. Verification
Some applications are selected for verification. Selected students must submit tax transcripts and a verification worksheet before aid is disbursed.

## 6. Appeals
Students who lose aid eligibility due to SAP may file a written appeal documenting extenuating circumstances. See the Appeals and Grievance Policy for procedures and deadlines.
