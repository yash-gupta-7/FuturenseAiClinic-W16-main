# Appeals and Grievance Policy

**Document owner:** Office of the Provost
**Last updated:** Fall 2025

## 1. Admission Decision Appeals
An applicant denied admission may appeal **within 30 days** of the decision. An appeal must present **significant new academic information** not available at the time of the original review (for example, a completed final term with improved grades, or a new standardized test score). Appeals that merely restate the original application are not reviewed.

## 2. Financial Aid (SAP) Appeals
A student who loses aid eligibility for failing Satisfactory Academic Progress may appeal in writing, documenting extenuating circumstances (illness, family emergency, etc.) and a plan to regain good standing. SAP appeals are due **within 15 business days** of the eligibility notice.

## 3. Grade Grievances
A student contesting a final grade must first discuss the grade with the instructor. If unresolved, the student may file a formal grievance with the department chair within **30 days of grade posting**, and then to the college dean if still unresolved.

## 4. General Grievances
Non-academic grievances (for example, a service or conduct concern) are submitted to the Office of the Dean of Students. The University prohibits retaliation against any person who files a good-faith grievance.

## 5. Decision and Finality
Each appeal or grievance receives a written decision. The decision of the final reviewing office (Provost or designee) is final and concludes the University's internal process.
